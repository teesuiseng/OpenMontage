Summary of Key Findings: Fructose Impairs Brown Adipogenesis via Thyroid Hormone Resistance
Background
Excessive fructose consumption is linked to metabolic diseases, but its effects on brown adipocytes—key regulators of energy homeostasis—remain poorly understood.

Main Findings
1. Fructose Impairs Brown Adipocyte Differentiation
Reduced lipid droplet size and number

Downregulated adipogenic markers: PPARγ, FABP4

Suppressed the brown adipocyte identity protein UCP-1

No significant changes in total protein or DNA content

2. Fructose Induces Thyroid Hormone Resistance
T3 (THR ligand) and resmetirom (THRβ agonist) dose-dependently increased UCP-1 in glucose medium

Neither agent restored UCP-1 expression in fructose medium → thyroid hormone resistance

3. Mechanism of THR Dysregulation
Fructose specifically downregulated THRα1β1 (not RXR heterodimer partner)

Mechanistically: ubiquitin-mediated degradation of THR

Increased THR ubiquitination in fructose environment

4. THR Overexpression Does Not Rescue UCP-1 in Fructose
Overexpressing THR increased UCP-1 in glucose (as expected)

Failed to restore UCP-1 in fructose → indicates a defect in THR transcriptional regulation

5. Lipid Dynamics
THR overexpression in glucose: reduced lipid droplet size (high turnover)

THR overexpression in fructose: no change in lipid droplets → impaired lipid remodeling

6. Secretome Proteomics Reveals Network Disruption
Fructose altered secretome by:

Increasing: glycolysis/TCA cycle proteins, mRNA processing, protein folding/chaperones (HSP90B, BiP), 14-3-3E signaling

Decreasing: redox defense proteins (e.g., Prdx6) → increased oxidative burden

THR overexpression in fructose:

Reduced glycolysis/TCA proteins

Decreased chaperone proteins → increased risk of protein misfolding

Proposed Mechanism
Fructose:

Enhances THR ubiquitination → proteasomal degradation

Induces oxidative stress (reduced Prdx6 secretion)

May oxidatively modify the zinc-finger DNA-binding domain of THR

Impairs THR-TRE binding → transcriptional silencing of UCP-1

Disrupts THR/RXR complex function → thyroid hormone resistance

Conclusions
Fructose is a potent inhibitor of brown adipogenesis

Induces thyroid hormone resistance via THR dysfunction

Disrupts energy metabolism, redox protection, and proteostasis

Links dietary fructose to impaired BAT function and metabolic imbalance

Significance
This study identifies a novel mechanism connecting dietary fructose to BAT dysfunction through THR-mediated transcriptional regulation, with implications for obesity, diabetes, and NAFLD pathogenesis.
